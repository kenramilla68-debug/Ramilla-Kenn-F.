Festivals in Negros Occidental

Explore the vibrant festivals and cultural celebrations of Negros Occidental.

PANAAD SA NEGROS

  • Negros Occidental is known for its many festivals, with the primary provincial event being the Panaad sa Negros Festival, often dubbed the "mother" of all local festivals. Major city festivals include the renowned MassKara Festival in Bacolod City and the historical Cinco de Noviembre Festival in Bago and Silay.

Major Provincial Festival Panaad sa Negros Festival: Held annually in April at the Panaad Park and Sports Complex in Bacolod City, this event gathers all 13 cities and 19 municipalities of the province to showcase their unique local festivals, history, culture, and products in themed pavilions. Prominent City Festivals MassKara Festival (Bacolod City): Known as the "City of Smiles," Bacolod celebrates this vibrant festival every October, with the highlight weekend typically falling on the third weekend of the month nearest to October 19 (Charter Anniversary). The festival features colorful masks, lively street dancing, and a grand electric parade. Dinagsa Ati-atihan Festival (Cadiz City): A week-long celebration held on the last Sunday of January in honor of the patron saint, Señor Santo Niño, featuring "Ati" tribes in colorful costumes and street dancing. Pintaflores Festival (San Carlos City): Celebrated on November 5, this festival is known for street dancers with painted body art resembling flowers, commemorating the city's history and the feast of St. Charles Borromeo.
